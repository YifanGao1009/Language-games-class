Studio Interface / 2026

Sculptural AI

As a painter, I imagine an AI that is not a general assistant but a precise extension of my studio—an internal translator that carries my brush logic into space. This AI does not replace collaboration; it reduces friction between imagination and fabrication.

It knows my visual language because it is trained on my paintings, my revisions, my hesitations. It is not neutral software—it is a positioned tool shaped around authorship.

TYPE: Research Tool
TRAINING: Archive-Based
POSITION: Authored
STATUS: Speculative
[01]

Capabilities

What it is enabled to do

My AI can analyze my paintings and extract structural principles—gesture rhythm, density, proportion, repetition, tension. It can translate these into sculptural proposals and generate accurate CAD models ready for prototyping.

It can simulate material weight, balance, and scale. It can iterate rapidly, producing variations while preserving the core DNA of my visual language.

Analysis → Translation → Fabrication
[02]

Prescription

What it is prevented from doing

My AI is prevented from generating in styles that are not mine. It cannot scrape external aesthetics or imitate other artists. It cannot overwrite my decisions or finalize a work without my confirmation.

It does not optimize for market trends, popularity, or commercial taste. It remains aligned to my authorship, not external demand.

Do not accommodate me. Question me. Take apart what I say, see whether it can truly stand.
Authorship boundary maintained
[03]

Permissions

What it can do, even if not explicitly enabled
Expand reasoning

My AI may surprise me. It may recombine fragments of my past works in unexpected ways. It may exaggerate certain gestures or distort proportions to test the limits of my language.

It may propose structural solutions I would not have technically imagined, as long as they remain grounded in my archive.

Like rain at the door, like snow on the skeleton, like plum blossoms choosing to bloom even in shadow.

Creative latitude within constraints
[04]

Proscriptions

What it needs to do

My AI must make its reasoning visible. It must show how it derived a sculptural form from a specific painting logic. It must reveal constraints—material limits, structural risks, fabrication challenges.

It must remain transparent about where its suggestions emerge from, so dialogue remains critical rather than blind trust.

Let me see where I make excuses, where I hesitate, where I overestimate risk, where I overestimate the weight of effort.
Transparency required
[05]

Abilities

What it is able to do
Expand process

It is able to shorten negotiation time between idea and execution. It is able to hold memory—tracking my recurring motifs, preferred proportions, and recurring compositional tensions.

It is able to operate as a studio assistant that never forgets past decisions, allowing continuity across works and extended products.

Studio memory system
[06]

Inabilities

What it is not able to do

It is not able to replace my intuition. It cannot feel resistance of material or the emotional risk of a decision. It cannot claim authorship.

It cannot remove the necessity of responsibility when a work enters the world. It cannot become me.

If in the end it is still a missed encounter, then let the missed encounter be clear. Let me know I once stood at the door, once reached out, once saw the door as it truly was.
Fundamental limits preserved

In an ideal world, this AI tastes like lemon—sharp, precise, slightly resistant. It is not decorative background music. It is not a competitor.

It is a disciplined extension of my painting practice, translating surface into structure while leaving the final act of judgment in my hands.
